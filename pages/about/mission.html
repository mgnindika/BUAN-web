Our Mission

What We Stand For

The Peter Callais Institute for Business Analytics at Loyola University New Orleans is committed to honoring Peter Callais' legacy of leadership, innovation, and service. We educate and empower students, professionals, and organizations through rigorous analytics training, interdisciplinary collaboration, and industry partnerships. Guided by Jesuit values, we cultivate ethical, innovative, and socially responsible leaders who use the power of data to advance business, strengthen communities, and inspire positive change.
Our Vision

Where We Are Going

To be a nationally recognized hub for ethical and impactful business analytics education and research — shaping future leaders who harness data to drive innovation, foster inclusion, and create transformative solutions for business and society.

Our Purpose

Why We Exist

Loyola University is uniquely positioned to produce well-rounded Business Analytics professionals using programs from the College of Business (COB), starting with our Business Analytics major and other COB areas of focus, such as AI, Ethical Decision Making, and Business Law, combined with Computer Science programs of the College of Arts and Sciences.

The Business Analytics Institute (BAI) aims to empower the next generation of data-driven decision-makers by providing comprehensive education and training in business analytics. Funded to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application within diverse industries, the institute will cater to undergraduate and graduate students, professionals seeking to enhance their analytical skills, and organizations looking to upskill their workforce.

Rooted in Tradition

The Jesuit Connection

Loyola University New Orleans, founded by the Society of Jesus, has guided students to "be men and women for and with others" for over a century. The Callais Institute extends this tradition into the data age.

We believe analytics is not morally neutral — the choices we make in data collection, modeling, and interpretation have real human consequences. Our curriculum embeds ethics throughout, not as an afterthought.
