Our Mission

Democratizing Data Literacy

The Callais Institute believes that data literacy is a civic skill — as fundamental as reading and writing for participation in 21st-century society. That's why we invest in open educational resources that any instructor, at any level, can use without cost.

Our Data Science Education initiative develops and distributes curricula, datasets, project templates, and pedagogical frameworks for teaching analytics. Resources are designed for high school teachers, community college instructors, and university faculty.

All materials are released under Creative Commons licensing and are free to download, adapt, and redistribute with attribution.

K–12 Outreach

Bringing Analytics into Schools

The Institute partners directly with New Orleans and Louisiana K–12 schools to bring data literacy programming into classrooms. Our K–12 education team provides professional development workshops for teachers, guest classroom visits, and structured curriculum support.

We prioritize schools in historically underserved communities, with particular focus on Title I schools in Orleans and Jefferson parishes.

  • ✓ Teacher PD workshops (full-day and half-day formats)
  • ✓ LOYNO student guest instructors for classroom visits
  • ✓ Curriculum co-design with your school's CS or math department
  • ✓ Free access to all Callais Institute educational materials
  • ✓ Pipeline to the AAI4HC summer program for top students
