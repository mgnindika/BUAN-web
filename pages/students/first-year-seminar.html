Course Overview

What Is the First Year Seminar?

The First Year Seminar (BUAN 1010) is a one-credit, pass/fail course required for all incoming Business Analytics majors. It meets weekly throughout the Fall semester and is designed not as a technical course, but as an orientation to the analytics profession and the Callais Institute community.

Students engage with guest practitioners, tour Institute facilities, complete a short team analytics challenge, and build relationships with faculty, GRAs, and upper-class mentors. The seminar sets the tone for your entire undergraduate journey.

All first-year BUAN majors are automatically enrolled. No prerequisites. No prior experience required.

Weekly Sessions

What We Cover

Each weekly session is 75 minutes and combines short presentations, hands-on activities, and discussion. Below is a representative sample of topics covered throughout the semester.

  • ✓
    Welcome to the Analytics Profession

    An overview of data roles, salary ranges, and the industries hiring analytics talent in 2025 and beyond.

  • ✓
    Tour: Impacts Lab & Data Infrastructure

    Hands-on orientation to the Institute's computing lab, databases, and data science tools.

  • ✓
    What Is a Data Problem? Framing the Question

    Case-based session on structuring business problems as analytics questions using real examples from healthcare, finance, and nonprofits.

  • ✓
    Guest Practitioner: A Day in the Life

    An industry data professional shares their career journey, daily workflows, and advice for incoming students.

  • ✓
    Ethics in Analytics: What Can Go Wrong

    Real-world examples of algorithmic bias, privacy violations, and the responsibility of the data professional.

  • ✓
    Intro to Excel & Data Storytelling

    A light hands-on introduction to spreadsheet-based analysis and how to communicate findings visually.

  • ✓
    Team Analytics Challenge

    Groups of 3–4 tackle a real local dataset and present findings to a panel of faculty and industry judges.

  • ✓
    Your 4-Year Analytics Roadmap

    Academic advising, certification paths, internship timelines, and how to make the most of your BUAN degree.

Seminar Format

  • → 14 weekly sessions — 75 minutes each
  • → 3 guest speakers — industry practitioners each semester
  • → 1 team project — collaborative analytics challenge with real data
  • → 1 reflection journal — submitted at end of semester
  • → Peer mentorship — matched with a junior or senior BUAN student

Instructor

The seminar is co-facilitated by the Institute's Graduate Research Assistants (GRAs) and guest faculty, with oversight from the Program Director.

Faculty and industry professionals rotate through as guest presenters and project judges throughout the semester.
