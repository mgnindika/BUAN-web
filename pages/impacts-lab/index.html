About the Lab

Analytics in Service of Community

The Impacts Lab is the Callais Institute's hands-on analytics consulting arm. Teams of 3–5 upper-level BUAN students work alongside faculty supervisors to tackle real data problems for nonprofit organizations, government agencies, and social enterprises in the New Orleans region.

Projects span 8–12 weeks per semester, covering everything from data cleaning and statistical analysis to predictive modeling and dashboard development. Every engagement ends with a client-ready deliverable and a public presentation.

For students, the Impacts Lab is often the most formative experience of their degree — a chance to work with messy, real-world data and see analytics make a tangible difference.

The Process

From Intake to Deliverable

Every Impacts Lab project follows a structured engagement model — rigorous enough for real results, flexible enough for diverse challenges.

1

Intake & Scoping

Client submits a project brief. Faculty and lab leads assess scope, feasibility, and data availability.

2

Team Assignment

3–5 student analysts matched to the project based on skills and interests. Faculty supervisor assigned.

3

Discovery Sprint

Kickoff meeting with client. Data ingested, cleaned, and explored. Problem defined precisely.

4

Analysis & Modeling

Core analytical work: statistical analysis, visualization, modeling, and insight generation.

5

Deliverable & Presentation

Final report, dashboard, or dataset delivered. Public presentation at semester's end.

Featured Work

Recent Projects

Completed Healthcare

Community Health Needs Assessment

Client: New Orleans Health Dept. | Fall 2024 | 4 students

Analyzed BRFSS survey data and ZIP-level health outcomes to identify neighborhoods with highest unmet healthcare needs. Deliverable: interactive Tableau dashboard used for grant application.

Completed Education

Early Warning System for Student Attrition

Client: KIPP New Orleans | Spring 2024 | 5 students

Built a logistic regression model predicting student withdrawal risk from attendance, grade, and behavioral data. Model achieved 81% accuracy and was integrated into counselor workflows.

Completed Food Access

Food Desert Mapping & Pantry Optimization

Client: Second Harvest Food Bank | Fall 2024 | 3 students

Geospatial analysis of USDA food access data and census demographics to identify optimal locations for 3 new pantry sites across southeast Louisiana.

In Progress Environment

Wetlands Loss Trend Analysis

Client: Coastal Protection Authority | Spring 2025 | 4 students

Time-series analysis of satellite land cover data to quantify wetlands loss rates by basin and model projected loss under current vs. restoration scenarios.

In Progress Housing

Affordable Housing Displacement Risk

Client: Greater New Orleans Housing Alliance | Spring 2025 | 5 students

Machine learning model predicting displacement risk for subsidized housing residents using property assessments, rental index trends, and infrastructure investment data.

Completed Public Safety

Crime Hotspot Predictive Analysis

Client: NOPD Data Unit | Fall 2023 | 4 students

Spatiotemporal analysis of 5 years of NOPD open data to identify crime concentration patterns and seasonal trends, informing patrol resource allocation.

Work With Us

Submit a Project Request

Tell us about your organization's data challenge. Lab coordinators review all requests and will reach out within 5 business days to discuss feasibility and next steps.

Projects are accepted on a rolling basis for Fall and Spring semesters. Spring intake closes January 15; Fall intake closes August 15.

What makes a good project?

  • ✓A clear question or decision to be informed by data
  • ✓Access to a dataset (or willingness to collect one)
  • ✓A named point of contact who can meet bi-weekly
  • ✓Realistic scope for an 8–12 week student project
